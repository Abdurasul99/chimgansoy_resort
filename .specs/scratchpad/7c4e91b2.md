## Phase 1: Requirements Discovery

### Task Overview

- **Initial User Prompt**: “теперь твоя задача подготовить полную документацию для задачи проекта”.
- **Current Description**: placeholder only; the previous design discussion supplies the intended redesign direction.
- **Task Type**: feature/redesign.
- **Complexity**: **L**. The work spans positioning, information architecture, booking semantics, shared navigation, three locales, responsive states, accessibility, performance, SEO, analytics and regression coverage. It is bounded because it does not introduce a new commerce or accommodation product.

### Problem Definition (Step-by-Step Analysis)

**Step 1 — Surface request.** Prepare an implementation-ready specification for redesigning the site/homepage.

**Step 2 — Actual outcome sought.** Make the public experience immediately and truthfully communicate that CHIMGAN DARBAZA is a day-use mountain venue, then help a guest understand the offer and start a reservation request with minimal friction.

**Step 3 — Business value.** The current experience sends conflicting hotel/accommodation signals through “arrival/departure”, rooms, an intro gate and a season switch. This can create false expectations, delay access to the offer and dilute the reservation CTA. A focused day-use homepage should improve qualified booking starts, reduce clarification work for the operator and build a differentiated brand around an authentic Uzbek mountain day.

**Step 4 — Stakeholder value.**

- Prospective families and groups get a truthful, fast and understandable path from discovery to request.
- Russian-, Uzbek- and English-speaking guests receive equivalent information.
- Mobile guests can act without overlays or dense content blocking the booking path.
- Reservation staff receive requests with the visit date and group size already supplied.
- The operator and marketing team get a distinctive “modern Uzbek mountain dacha” identity without advertising paused inventory.
- Future accommodation stakeholders retain paused glamping/cottage assets for later reactivation without exposing them in this release.

**Step 5 — Immediate vs future capabilities.** This release covers the homepage story, day-use booking entry and the shared navigation surfaced from the homepage. Future releases may reintroduce overnight stays, add Exely online inventory, publish a richer kitchen menu, commission new photography or create a broader visual identity system only after business approval.

**Step 6 — Constraints.** The venue is open daily 08:00–18:00, is 45 minutes from Tashkent at 1700 m, and a topchan accommodates up to eight guests. Overnight stays remain paused. Existing request handling remains the fallback; the redesign must not imply instant availability or payment. All content must have RU/UZ/EN parity, use approved real-property imagery, remain accessible, and preserve performance on mobile connections.

**Root problem.** The public homepage does not consistently align its story and conversion flow with the venue’s current day-use business, so guests can misinterpret the product and encounter avoidable friction before they can request a topchan.

### Scope

**Included**

- Homepage visual and content direction: authentic “modern Uzbek mountain dacha”.
- A concise homepage of no more than seven primary content sections after the hero.
- Immediate hero access without a blocking logo/splash sequence.
- Single-day topchan request entry using visit date and 1–8 guests.
- Preservation of those selections through the existing request flow.
- Day-use pricing, inclusions, cooking, food, atmosphere, reviews/FAQ, directions and final CTA.
- Homepage-visible desktop/mobile header and footer navigation, including removal of public overnight links.
- Removal of the guest-facing season control; automatic season presentation may remain.
- FAQ placement/content alignment so it cannot obstruct booking.
- RU/UZ/EN parity, responsive behavior, accessibility, performance, SEO and analytics requirements.

**Explicitly excluded**

- Opening, deleting or redesigning glamping/cottage detail inventory; those paused assets remain available for a future release.
- Exely onboarding/widget customization, live availability, instant booking, online payment, cancellation or overnight check-in flows.
- A database, CMS, authentication or customer account capability.
- Changing notification providers, mailbox routing or operational reservation handling beyond carrying the day-use fields.
- Commissioning a photoshoot, generating synthetic property imagery or inventing unavailable facilities.
- Full rebranding, legal-policy rewrite, unrelated secondary-page redesign, DNS/deployment/infrastructure work.

### Ambiguous Areas and Defaults

| Ambiguous element | Reasoning | Default applied |
|---|---|---|
| “Simplified booking” fields | No approved inventory taxonomy beyond a topchan is defined. | Show exactly two guest-facing selectors: visit date and guests (1–8); the CTA requests a topchan. |
| Shorter homepage | “Short” is subjective. | Limit to hero plus at most seven primary sections, following the defined content sequence. |
| Seasonal design | Season-aware imagery exists, but a public toggle feels technical. | Allow automatic seasonal presentation; remove the guest-facing switch and keep the same information architecture year-round. |
| New photography | Desired lifestyle categories are known, but procurement is not part of a web implementation. | Use only approved real venue images currently supplied; photography production is out of scope. |
| Price source | Prices can change operationally. | Display values and weekday/weekend grouping from the operator-approved price source at release time. |
| Booking result | Current operations reply manually and delivery configuration may vary. | Describe the action as a request, preserve the existing fallback route, and do not promise immediate confirmation or a fixed response time. |

No blocking clarification is required for business analysis. Product/translation/photo approval remains an implementation dependency rather than an unresolved scope choice.

## Phase 2: Concept Extraction

### Key Concepts Identified

**Step 1 — Actors.** Prospective guest, family/group organizer, reservation operator, content/marketing owner, translator/localization reviewer, accessibility/QA reviewer and future accommodation product owner.

**Step 2 — Actions and behaviors.** Discover the day-use proposition; inspect prices/inclusions; select a date and guest count; continue to a prefilled request; learn what the day looks like; inspect food/cooking/directions; search FAQ; contact the venue; switch locale; navigate with touch, keyboard or assistive technology.

**Step 3 — Data entities.** Locale, visit date, guest count, request type, approved price list, operating facts, inclusions, venue imagery and alt text, navigation targets, contact/directions data, FAQ entries and anonymous conversion events.

**Step 4 — Constraints.** One-day visit only, 08:00–18:00, maximum eight guests per topchan, no public overnight promotion, no claim of live inventory, three locales, real imagery, mobile-first readability and existing request fallback.

**Step 5 — Implicit assumptions.** The approved pricing/contact content is authoritative; users may arrive on any locale; the majority of high-intent sessions may be mobile; topchan is the current primary bookable format; third-party maps/notifications may be unavailable; the venue owns or has permission to publish supplied photos.

**Conclusion.** The feature is a truthful, localized discovery-to-request journey centered on a single-day visit, supported by a restrained brand system and resilient content fallbacks.

### Concept Summary

- **Actors**: guests/groups, reservation operator, marketing/content owner, translators, QA/accessibility reviewers, future accommodation owner.
- **Actions/Behaviors**: learn, compare prices, request a day, ask questions, get directions and contact staff.
- **Data Entities**: visit date, guests, prices, schedule, location, images, FAQ and locale.
- **Constraints**: day-use only, up to eight guests, no live availability promise, RU/UZ/EN parity, approved content only.

### Implicit Assumptions

- A date selected on the homepage is a requested visit date, not a confirmed reservation.
- A group larger than eight should contact the venue or submit an inquiry rather than silently selecting an invalid count.
- The homepage can use anchors and existing destination pages, but every public navigation item must resolve to a valid target.
- Removing links to paused accommodation does not authorize deletion of its retained pages/content.
- Seasonal effects are decorative and optional; the core proposition, layout and booking path stay stable year-round.

### Scope Analysis

- **In Scope**: homepage and its shared navigation surfaces; end-to-end preservation of day-use booking values; relevant FAQ/directions fallback; visual/content requirements and quality gates.
- **Out of Scope**: live inventory/payment, accommodation reopening, provider changes, asset production, unrelated pages.
- **Boundary Cases**: past/empty date, guest count outside 1–8, 320 px viewport, floating FAQ collision, slow/failed images, failed map embed, keyboard-only use, reduced-motion mode, mixed-language content and obsolete overnight copy.

## Phase 3: Requirements Analysis

### Functional Requirements Analysis

1. **Entrance and positioning**: users must see content immediately; the hero names the brand, day-use offer and essential facts without a splash gate.
2. **Day-use request**: two explicit controls collect visit date and 1–8 guests; the action is clearly a request for a topchan, not room booking or instant confirmation.
3. **Continuity**: valid selections survive navigation to the request form and current operational delivery remains intact.
4. **Concise story**: the page uses a fixed narrative sequence with no more than seven primary sections after the hero.
5. **Truthful content**: prices/facts match approved sources; overnight, unbuilt or unavailable facilities are absent from the homepage, navigation and surfaced FAQ.
6. **Authentic art direction**: real property imagery depicts topchan/kurpacha, food or fire, people/shared use and the mountain setting; the palette remains warm, restrained and recognizably brand-led.
7. **Usable navigation and help**: navigation targets are relevant and valid; the season control is absent; FAQ never blocks the booking CTA; directions/contact information remains useful if an embed fails.
8. **Localized resilience**: RU/UZ/EN offer equivalent meaning and appropriate terminology; failure states and fallbacks remain understandable.

### Functional Requirements

- Immediate content access without a timed intro, modal or scroll lock.
- Hero with day-use proposition, key facts and primary request action.
- Exactly one requested visit date and one guest count (1–8) on the homepage entry form.
- Validation of empty/past dates and unsupported guest counts.
- Prefill continuity to the request form and no false instant-confirmation language.
- Defined, shorter information architecture and final conversion opportunity.
- Approved price, schedule, location, capacity and contact content.
- No public accommodation promotion or obsolete FAQ topics.
- Relevant, valid navigation with no guest-facing season switch.
- Non-obstructive FAQ and resilient map/image behavior.
- Complete RU/UZ/EN parity.

### Non-Functional Requirements

- **Accessibility**: WCAG 2.2 AA for the redesigned experience; semantic structure, keyboard operation, visible focus, accessible labels/errors, 44×44 CSS px touch targets, contrast and reduced-motion support.
- **Performance**: median of three mobile Lighthouse production-build runs achieves Performance ≥90, Accessibility ≥95, LCP ≤2.5 s and CLS ≤0.10 under Lighthouse mobile defaults.
- **Responsiveness**: no horizontal overflow or clipped/overlapping controls from 320 px through 1440 px; core checks at 320, 390, 768, 1024 and 1440 px.
- **Compatibility**: latest two stable releases of Chrome, Safari, Firefox and Edge; touch and keyboard paths.
- **SEO**: one descriptive H1, indexable localized homepages, correct locale alternates/canonicals, and retained valid structured data.
- **Privacy/measurement**: record booking starts and successful request completion without sending name, phone, message or other personal form values in analytics.
- **Reliability**: slow/failed nonessential imagery and map embeds must not remove offer, price, address, phone or CTA access.

### Constraints and Assumptions

- Business facts: daily 08:00–18:00, 45 minutes from Tashkent, 1700 m, up to eight guests per topchan.
- Overnight inventory remains paused and retained but not publicly promoted.
- The request form remains the operational fallback before and after any future booking engine launch.
- Prices, translations, photos and claims require operator approval before release.
- Existing notification delivery configuration is a dependency and is not redesigned here.
- Existing supplied venue images are the release asset pool unless the operator adds approved originals.

### Dependencies

- Operator-approved day-use prices, inclusions, operating facts and contact details.
- Approved RU, UZ and EN copy with equivalent meaning.
- Approved real venue images and usage rights.
- Existing booking-request destination and notification routing.
- Map/directions URL plus a plain-text address and phone fallback.
- Analytics environment for anonymous funnel measurement.

### Measurable Outcomes

- 0 blocking intro/splash screens before the homepage is usable.
- 0 public homepage/header/footer links or sections that sell rooms, cottages, glamping or overnight check-in.
- 100% of valid homepage booking starts carry visit date and guest count to the request form.
- 100% of redesigned user-facing copy has RU/UZ/EN variants with no unintended fallback-language leakage.
- At most seven primary content sections after the hero.
- 0 overlap between FAQ controls and visible booking controls/CTAs at the specified mobile breakpoints.
- Lighthouse and accessibility thresholds meet the stated targets.
- Analytics can report homepage booking starts and successful request submissions without collecting PII.

### User Scenarios

1. **Primary booking flow**: A mobile guest lands on `/ru`, immediately understands this is a day visit, chooses a future date and 1–8 guests, selects the topchan request CTA and sees both values prefilled on the request form.
2. **Price-first comparison**: A guest compares the approved Mon–Thu and Fri–Sun price columns, sees what is included and what can be rented/bought, then starts the same request flow.
3. **Experience discovery**: A guest scans how the day unfolds, sees authentic topchan, cooking, food, people and mountain imagery, and reaches the final CTA without accommodation messaging.
4. **FAQ/contact alternative**: A guest opens/searches the FAQ without losing access to the booking CTA; if no answer exists, localized contact actions are available.
5. **Directions fallback**: If the map does not load, the guest can still read/copy the address and use a valid map/contact link.
6. **Validation error**: A guest submits no date, a past date or an unsupported guest count and receives a localized, programmatically associated error while valid input is retained.
7. **Localization**: A guest visits RU, UZ or EN and receives equivalent navigation, labels, facts, price meaning, errors, FAQ and CTA behavior.
8. **Accessible/reduced-motion use**: A keyboard or assistive-technology user, or a user requesting reduced motion, can navigate and complete the request entry without focus loss, forced animation or hidden controls.

### Error Scenarios

- Missing/past visit date: prevent continuation and identify the date field with a localized error.
- Guest count outside 1–8 or missing: prevent continuation and identify the guest field; provide a contact path for larger groups.
- Invalid/stale booking parameters on the destination: do not crash; show safe empty/default controls and require valid input.
- FAQ query with no result: display a localized no-result state and contact fallback.
- Map/embed failure: keep address, schedule, phone and external directions action visible.
- Image failure/slow load: preserve readable foreground content, dimensions and CTA interaction without layout shift.

## Phase 4: Draft Output

### Synthesis Reasoning

**Step 1 — Core value.** Align the digital promise with the revenue-generating day-use product and shorten the path to a qualified request.

**Step 2 — Must-have vs future.** Must-have: clear day-use proposition, concise authentic story, single-date flow, no overnight leakage, usable navigation/FAQ, three locales and quality gates. Future: accommodations, live Exely inventory/payment, new photography and broader rebranding.

**Step 3 — Testable criteria.** Twenty-nine criteria cover functional flow, content truth, edge cases, responsive behavior, accessibility, performance, SEO, analytics and regression.

**Step 4 — Scope boundaries.** Paused accommodation is hidden, not deleted; operational notification providers and unrelated pages are preserved; live booking and asset production are excluded.

**Step 5 — Communication structure.** Lead with the business mismatch, identify stakeholders/goals, state scope and assumptions, document scenarios, then provide individually numbered Given/When/Then criteria and Definition of Done.

**Conclusion.** The refined specification answers why the redesign matters, what observable experience must be delivered, and who benefits, without prescribing component or framework implementation.

### Refined Description Summary

Build a concise, mobile-first homepage that positions CHIMGAN DARBAZA as a day-use mountain venue and expresses the “modern Uzbek mountain dacha” through real scenes of topchans, kurpacha, fire, food, people and landscape. Remove hotel-like and technical friction—blocking intro, arrival/departure semantics, public overnight promotion and the season switch—while keeping paused accommodation assets intact for later.

The main conversion journey collects a single visit date and 1–8 guests, carries these values into the existing request form and makes clear that staff confirmation follows. The page tells a bounded story through prices/inclusions, a day narrative, food/atmosphere, directions, trust/FAQ and a final CTA, with equivalent RU/UZ/EN experiences and measurable accessibility/performance quality.

### Scope Summary

- **Included**: homepage, its shared navigation surfaces, day-request prefill continuity, relevant FAQ/directions fallbacks, localization and quality gates.
- **Excluded**: accommodation reopening/deletion, Exely integration, payments/live inventory, provider/infrastructure changes, photo production, full rebrand and unrelated pages.

### User Scenarios Summary

Eight scenarios are documented: primary request, price-first comparison, experience discovery, FAQ/contact, directions fallback, validation failure, localization and accessible/reduced-motion use.

All Critical and High gaps are addressed in the final draft.
